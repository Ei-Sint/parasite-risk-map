Assumptions & Limitations

📊 Data Sources

  • Weather data is sourced from Open-Meteo API and updates in real-time
  • Risk calculations are based on established veterinary research on parasite lifecycles
  • Farm locations are approximate coordinates for demonstration purposes

🔬 Risk Calculation Assumptions

  • Temperature above 10°C increases parasite activity
  • Higher rainfall (>5mm/week) creates favorable conditions for larvae survival
  • Humidity above 60% extends parasite lifespan on pasture
  • Spring and Autumn are peak risk seasons for most parasites

🐛 Parasite-Specific Factors

  • Liver Fluke: Requires wet conditions and mud snail intermediate hosts
  • Lungworm: Thrives in warm, humid conditions; spread via slugs and snails
  • Gut Worms: Peak during grazing season with warm, moist pastures
  • Coccidia: Highest risk in young animals during spring turnout

⚠️ Limitations

  • This tool provides estimates only and should not replace professional veterinary advice
  • Local microclimate variations may affect actual risk levels
  • Stocking density, grazing history, and previous treatments are not factored in
  • The model does not account for anthelmintic resistance patterns
  • 7-day rainfall data may not capture longer-term moisture trends

✅ Recommendations

  • Always conduct faecal egg counts (FEC) before treatment decisions
  • Consult with your veterinarian for farm-specific parasite management plans
  • Use this tool as one part of an integrated parasite control strategy
  • Monitor livestock regularly for clinical signs regardless of risk level
